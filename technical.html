Jeppesen Aqequate Visibility Reference

Airlines may specify other than standard takeoff minimums. The minimums and visual reference requirements will be contained in paragraphs C056 and C078 of their Ops Specs. For any operation in which the visbility is less than 1/2SM, Ops Specs paragraph C078 states the "visual reference" requirements. Visual references include various lighting or centerline markings.
C078. IFR Lower Than Standard Takeoff Minimums. All Airports

Runway visual range (RVR) reports, when available for a particual runway, shall be used for all takeoff operations on that runway. All takeoff operations, based on RVR, must use the RVR reports from the locations along the runway specified in this paragraph. When takeoff minimums are equal to or less than the applicable standard takeoff minimum, the certifcate holder is authorized to use the loower than standard takeoff minimums described below:

When no visual aids are available, visility or RVV (Runway Visibility Value) 1/4 statute mile may still be used, provided other runway markings or runway lighting provide pilots with adequate visual reference to continuously identiy the take off surface and maintain directional control throughout the takeoff run.

(Almost any runway markings will work. CL lights, CL marking, runway edge lights, etc. A snow-covered runway without visbile lighting wouldn't qualify. When you go below the 1/4sm or RVR1600' level you need specific lighting and/or markings which are listed with the takeoff minimums.)

(1) Visibility or runway visual value (RVV) 1/2 sm or touchdown zone RVR1600, provided at least one of the folllowing visual aids is available. The touchdown zone RVR report, if available, is controlling. The mid RVR reprot may be substituted for the touchdown zone RVR report if the touchdown zone RVR is not avilable.
(a) Operative high intensity runway lights (HIRL).
(b) Operative runway centerline lights (CL).
(c) Serviceable runway centerline markings (RCLM).
